# Application Materials Pipeline Standard v2

## Purpose

Produce a complete, job-specific application packet that is accurate, ATS-readable, visually polished, written in Geoffrey Hadfield's voice, and fully reviewable before submission. No pipeline can guarantee an ATS pass or interview; the goal is to maximize truthful alignment and remove avoidable failures.

## Required Inputs

1. Full job description from the official employer source when available.
2. Job ID, company, role, location, application URL, and publication date.
3. Master achievement bank with exact dates, tools, metrics, scope, and evidence strength.
4. Voice samples from Geoffrey's existing resumes and messages.
5. Sensitive-question policy and submission-authority policy.
6. Prior communications and the card's linear story.

## Hard Gates

A packet must stop before submission when any of the following is true:

- A required employer fact, date, degree, tool, or metric has no source.
- Two sources give different meanings to the same metric.
- A hard qualification gap is hidden or replaced by a derived claim.
- A sensitive/legal/self-identification question needs Geoffrey's answer.
- The portal requires an action the bot is not authorized to perform.
- The final resume fails text extraction, visual rendering, or manual review.
- The submission has no evidence capture or duplicate-action protection.

## Job-by-Job Workflow

### 1. Parse and classify the role

Extract responsibilities, hard qualifications, preferred qualifications, role level, domain, required tools, leadership expectations, and application questions. Separate:

- **Hard requirements:** tenure, work authorization, location, clearance, degree, required certification.
- **Optimizable requirements:** keywords, examples, bullet selection, summary, project ordering, messages, and answers.

### 2. Build a requirement-to-evidence matrix

For every major requirement, assign:

- Evidence source.
- Evidence strength: repeated, single-source, conflict, or missing.
- Best proof point.
- Resume section.
- Interview story.
- Verification action.

Never select a keyword without a supporting fact.

### 3. Select achievements

Use the smallest set of achievements that covers the role well. Prioritize:

1. Directly matching decisions and outcomes.
2. Recent work.
3. Repeated/source-strong metrics.
4. End-to-end ownership.
5. Leadership and stakeholder evidence.
6. Projects that close a current keyword gap.

Avoid stuffing every project into every resume.

### 4. Write the resume

Employer-facing resume rules:

- One or two pages; senior/technical roles may use two.
- Single column, normal body text, standard headings, no text boxes or skill bars.
- Contact information in the document body.
- Reverse chronological experience.
- Verb-first bullets: **Action + method/tool + decision/output + measured result**.
- No internal claim IDs, scores, agent trace, or "Target:" line.
- No first-person pronouns.
- No unsupported tenure totals or superlatives.
- Include role language naturally: product strategy, success metrics, experimentation, forecasting, instrumentation, roadmap, executive communication, responsible AI.

### 5. Write the cover letter

- 250-350 words, one page.
- Paragraph 1: role and the two strongest requirements matched.
- Paragraph 2: strongest business/analytics evidence with metrics.
- Paragraph 3: current work and distinctive proof, including responsible AI when relevant.
- Paragraph 4: direct close.
- No flattery, generic enthusiasm, company trivia, or repeated resume bullets without a narrative link.

### 6. Write outreach and answers

Prepare:

- LinkedIn connection request under 300 characters.
- Recruiter DM under 170 words.
- Recruiter email under 250 words.
- Hiring-manager note.
- 60-second pitch.
- Two follow-ups with a stop rule.
- 6-10 application/interview answers using Situation, Decision, Action, Result, and Learning.

### 7. Metric validation

Each metric record must contain:

- Exact metric name and unit.
- Baseline and comparison.
- Relative versus absolute change.
- Numerator/denominator when applicable.
- Time period and population.
- Candidate's contribution versus team contribution.
- Source and evidence strength.
- Allowed phrasing.

A conflict such as "12% engagement" versus "12% ROI" blocks the metric until resolved. Do not solve the conflict with a vague phrase.

### 8. Voice validation

The draft should sound direct, technical, and grounded. Flag or remove:

- passionate, thrilled, excited to, leverage, spearheaded, cutting-edge, seamless, dynamic environment, fast-paced, wealth of experience, proven track record, and exclamation marks.
- Empty claims such as "results-driven," "visionary," or "world-class" without evidence.
- Long sentences that combine more than one main achievement.

Compare at least five draft sentences with Geoffrey's source bullets. The verbs, sentence length, and level of detail should feel consistent.

### 9. ATS validation

Required checks:

- Plain-text extraction is in correct reading order.
- Name, email, phone, portfolio, GitHub, and LinkedIn are extractable.
- Standard headings are present.
- Dates and job titles parse correctly.
- No tables or text boxes in the employer-facing resume.
- Target keywords appear only when evidence-backed.
- File name contains candidate, company, and role.
- DOCX is the primary upload unless the portal explicitly asks for PDF.

### 10. Visual validation

Render every DOCX to page images and inspect every page at 100% zoom. Check:

- No clipping, overlap, orphan headings, broken bullets, or split job headings.
- Balanced page density.
- Consistent spacing, font sizes, color, and alignment.
- Hyperlinks work and are readable.
- Cover letter remains one page.

### 11. Review experience and linear story

The application card should show, in order:

1. Job discovered and scored.
2. Geoffrey selected it.
3. Requirements and gaps identified.
4. Achievements selected.
5. Resume, cover letter, answers, and outreach generated.
6. Validation results.
7. Review notes and each regeneration attempt.
8. Manual edits.
9. Submission evidence and email record.
10. Recruiter/interview follow-up.

Each row should have a one-line summary and expandable detail. Geoffrey must be able to edit every employer-facing section and regenerate from notes without losing prior history.

### 12. Submission and recordkeeping

Before the submit button is enabled:

- All hard validation errors are clear.
- Sensitive questions remain human-owned.
- Final resume and cover letter are visible.
- Portal answers are visible.
- Apply URL is official or verified.
- Duplicate-submission lock is active.
- Evidence capture is configured.

After submission, save the final files, job description, answers, confirmation number, timestamp, application URL, recruiter messages, and validation report. Send or store a complete application record.

## 100-Point Quality Rubric

- Evidence accuracy and traceability: 25
- Job alignment and prioritization: 20
- Impact and metric quality: 15
- Clarity and Geoffrey voice: 15
- ATS readability: 10
- Visual quality: 10
- Packet completeness and reviewability: 5

A score below 90 requires another pass. Any hard-gate failure blocks submission regardless of score.
